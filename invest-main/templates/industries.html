Why Invest Here

Investment Opportunity

Growth

A Region on the Rise

CPDA's regional population is projected to grow 4.9% to 36,020 by 2034, bringing 28,300+ workers into an expanding labour market. This growth trajectory makes the region an increasingly attractive location for businesses requiring stable workforce pipelines.

Common Questions

Industry FAQ

Answers to the questions we hear most from investors, businesses, and prospective residents.

Agriculture (8.1%) and manufacturing (6.8%) lead the regional economy by business share, but the region is deliberately diverse. Non-metallic mineral mining is the single largest export at 31.1% of regional exports. Healthcare, education, and public administration also contribute significantly to stable employment and export revenues.
CPDA provides a unified regional entry point for businesses and investors — coordinating across 16 member communities to assist with site selection, workforce connections, and introductions to local business networks. Our investment attraction sub-committee is developing a formal concierge service for prospective businesses.
Yes. The CPDA region is projected to grow 4.9% to 36,020 residents by 2034, with 28,300+ workers enabling expanding economic opportunity. Workforce planning is one of CPDA's four active sub-committee focus areas, ensuring regional labour needs are proactively addressed.
CPDA's industrial diversity is a key differentiator. Most rural Saskatchewan regions rely heavily on one or two sectors. CPDA's spread across agriculture, manufacturing, health, education, retail, and public administration creates economic resilience. The presence of Yorkton as a regional service hub — combined with an active regional alliance — makes coordinated development uniquely achievable.
Businesses can engage with CPDA through our Get Involved page. Whether you want to participate in sub-committee work, stay informed on regional developments, or explore formal membership, we welcome all businesses who share a stake in a stronger East-Central Saskatchewan.
