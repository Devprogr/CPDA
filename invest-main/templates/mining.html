Why Mining Here

World-Class Reserves.
Built to Supply the Globe.

Saskatchewan is not a peripheral mining region — it is the centre of the world's potash supply. Our CPDA communities sit within reach of Esterhazy, one of the highest-capacity mines on earth, meaning established supply chains, experienced workers, and a full business ecosystem are already in place.

For businesses outside the province, this represents a ready-made market for industrial services, workforce training, logistics, and technology — without the need to build from scratch.

Open Opportunities

Where Outside Businesses
Can Enter & Grow

You don't need to own a mine to benefit from mining. The industry creates hundreds of adjacent roles — procurement, training, maintenance, technology — all accessible to businesses willing to set up in or partner with the CPDA region.

Ask About Entry Points →
  • 01
    Industrial Services & Maintenance
    Ongoing maintenance contracts, seasonal shutdowns, and equipment repair create steady, predictable demand for skilled service providers year-round.
  • 02
    Supply Chain & Vendor Onboarding
    Major operations actively seek local suppliers for equipment, PPE, industrial chemicals, and materials — entry points exist for verified regional vendors.
  • 03
    Workforce Training & Safety
    Mines require ongoing safety certification, trade upgrading, and first-aid training — a growing and underserved niche for specialized training businesses.
  • 04
    Automation & Technology
    Predictive maintenance, sensor networks, and autonomous equipment are transforming mining — and our region needs the technology providers to match.
