Why Agriculture Here

Premium Farmland.
Ready Infrastructure.

Saskatchewan's dark fertile soils consistently produce world-class grain, oilseeds, and pulses. Our CPDA region sits at the centre of this agricultural heartland — with grain elevators, processing facilities, and transport connections already in place.

For outside investors and businesses, this means you're not building from zero. The market, the workers, the roads, and the buyers already exist. You just need to show up.

Grain & Oilseeds

Wheat, canola, barley, and flax grown across thousands of acres — with established elevator and export infrastructure to move product efficiently.

Pulse Crops

Lentils, peas, and chickpeas — Saskatchewan is the world's largest pulse exporter, and our region is a prime growing area for high-value crops.

Livestock

Cattle, hog, and poultry operations supply regional and national markets, with forage production supporting herds across our communities.

Precision agriculture, GPS-guided equipment, and drone-assisted crop management are reshaping how our producers operate — and where providers can enter.

Open Opportunities

Where Outside Businesses
Can Enter & Grow

Agriculture supports a vast ecosystem beyond the farm gate. Equipment, inputs, processing, storage, logistics, and digital tools are all growth areas where outside businesses can enter our region with minimal friction and strong demand.

Ask About Entry Points →
  • 01
    Agri-Input & Equipment Supply
    Seed, fertilizer, chemicals, and machinery — local farms represent consistent, high-volume purchasing power year-round.
  • 02
    Processing & Value-Add
    Primary grain processing, pulse cleaning, and specialty crop handling are underrepresented locally — high-demand opportunity for incoming processors.
  • 03
    Cold Storage & Logistics
    Refrigerated storage, grain handling, and transport coordination services are needed to connect our producers with premium markets.
  • 04
    Precision Ag Technology
    Drone services, soil sensors, yield mapping, and farm management software are being adopted rapidly — and providers are scarce regionally.
